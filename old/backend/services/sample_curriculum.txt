BUSINESS ENGLISH CURRICULUM

Module 1: Professional Scheduling
When scheduling a meeting, avoid informal language like "I wants to schedules".
Correct phrasing:
- "I would like to schedule a meeting with you."
- "Let's arrange a time to meet."
When mentioning tomorrow, do not use the preposition "for" if stating it directly as an adverb.
Correct: "I want to meet tomorrow."
Incorrect: "I want to meet for tomorrow."

Module 2: Email Sign-offs
Use "Best regards," or "Sincerely," for external clients.
Use "Best," or "Thanks," for internal team members.
