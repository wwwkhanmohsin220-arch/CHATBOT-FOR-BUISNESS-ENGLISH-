# Chapter 1: Business Communication

## De-escalating Conflict via Email

When dealing with an angry client or boss, the most important rule is to remain calm and professional. Do not mirror their emotional tone. Instead, acknowledge their frustration and immediately pivot to a solution.

For example, if a client is upset about a missed deadline, you should say: "I completely understand your frustration regarding the delay. Here is the exact timeline for when you will receive the final deliverables."

## Structuring a Presentation

A strong presentation always starts with a hook. You have exactly 30 seconds to capture the audience's attention before they check their phones. Start with a surprising statistic, a provocative question, or a brief story.

Always remember the rule of three: group your main points into three distinct sections. Humans naturally process and remember information presented in triads.
